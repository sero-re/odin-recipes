Palak Paneer

Palak Paneer - Spinach with Indian cottage cheese

Palak paneer is a popular Indian vegetarian dish consisting of paneer (Indian cheese) in a smooth, creamy and delicious spinach gravy. It is a classic curry dish from the north Indian cuisine made with fresh spinach, onions, spices, paneer, and herbs. Learn to make the best palak paneer at home, all without blanching spinach and overloading your dish with tons of spices.

Ingredients

  • 150g paneer - Indian cottage cheese
  • 100-120g palak - Spinach
  • 4 tbsp oil (1 tbsp oil and 1 tbsp butter)
  • 4 green chilies (deseeded, less spicy kind if preferred)
  • 90g onions, finely chopped
  • 360g tomatoes deseeded & chopped or pureed
  • 2 tsp ginger garlic paste
  • 1.5 tsp salt
  • 16-20 cashews nuts or 7 blanched almonds
  • 1-1.5 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves. Skip if not available)

    • Optional

  • 6 tbsp cream
  • 0.25 tsp cumin seeds
  • 4 green cardamoms
  • 2 inches of cinnamon
  • 4 cloves

Instructions

    Preparation

  1. Pluck only the young & tender spinach leaves and discard the stems asthey may leave a bitter taste. If using baby spinach you can use the stems as well
  2. Add them to a large pot of water. Rinse them well a few times & drain to a colander.
  3. Allow the water to drain completely, otherwise it will let out lots ofmoisture while cooking.
  4. Heat half tbsp oil in a pan. Saute green chilies, cashews, and spinach for 3 to 4 mins until the leaves wilt off thoroughly & raw smell of spinach has gone away.[ Or, another option is to blanch the palak in 4 cups of hot water with 1/4 tsp salt for 2 min. Then immerse in ice cold water. Drain completely.
  5. Cool this completely. Blend this along with 1/4 cup of water to a smooth puree. The puree should be smooth and thick. You may add 1 to 2 tbsp more water to help in blending.

    How to make palak paneer

  1. Heat 1 tbsp butter and half tbsp oil to the same pan. Once they melt, add cinnamon, cardamoms, cloves, and cumin seeds.
  2. When the spices begin to sizzle, add onions and fry till they turn transparent to golden.
  3. Saute ginger garlic paste for 1 to 2 min or until you begin to smell it nicely
  4. Add tomatoes with salt. Saute until they break down and turn mushy.
  5. Add garam masala & saute until the masala smells good. This may teake upto 2 min.
  6. Pour 3/4 cup water and cook covered until onions are completely soft. There should be some water left in the pan.
  7. Lower the flame, add kasuri methi and pureed spinach. Mix well and cook until it begins to bubble for about 2 to 3 mins. If the curry is too thick, you may add a few tbsp of hot water.
  8. Avoid overcooking. add paneer and mix well. Turn off and remove to a serving bowl. Optionally garnish with cream
  9. Serve palak paneer with naan, roti or Jeera rice.
