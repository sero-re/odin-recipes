Embutido - a Filipino meatloaf variety

Embutido is meatloaf Filipino style. Usually served in special occasions, but can be enjoyed any day. It's meatloaf made from ground pork made flavourful with raisins, carrots,onions. The middle usually contains slices of egg, some include hotdogs too. Others ingredients may include bell pepper, relish, and cheese.

Traditionally cooked by steaming, it can also be cooked through baking. After steaming, they can be frozen to be enjoyed at a later date. It can be served cold after thawing, but the majority prefer frying it up a little to give it a nice colour. It can be served and enjoyed with hot steamed rice with sweet chili or ketchup on the side.

Ingredients for about 6 servings

  • 500g ground pork
  • 2 eggs
  • 30g flour
  • 110g breadcrumbs
  • 1 medium sized carrot - diced or grated
  • 1 medium onion - finely minced
  • 1/4 cup green bell pepper - minced
  • 80-100g raisins - depending on how much you like raisins
  • 3 tablespoon tomato sauce or ketchup
  • 80g sweet pickle relish
  • 1/4 teaspoon pepper
  • 1-2 teaspoon of salt - preference
  • 6 pieces of Vienna sausage or 3 hotdogs
  • 2 hard boiled eggs - each cut into 4

Instructions

  1. Mix all ingedients in a large bowl except the last two - hard boiled eggs and sausage/hotdog.
  2. Cut 2 pieces of aluminum foil about 12 inches long each. Take half of the meat mixture and flatten it on of the foil, covering about a half. Don't forget to leave some space at the ends, about 2 inches each side.
  3. Place the sausages and hard boiled eggs on the middle of the flattened meat.
  4. Roll the foil so that the meat forms a cylinder or log, locking the sausage and egg in the center. Twist the ends of the foil to lock.
  5. Steam for 50 minutes.
Notes - this recipe makes 2 big rolls or 4 small ones
